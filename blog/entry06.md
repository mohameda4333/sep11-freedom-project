## Skills
Some skills I learned after finishing my freedom project are collaboration and problem decomposition.
### Collaboration
Collaboration with other people is an essential skill to learn especially when someone wants to teach themselves about something new or especially when building a project. I learned this skill as I learned to collaborate with my cousin. He taught me some functions and loops that I didn't know I could use in kaboom. Learning how to collaborate with people can help you a lot as it can help you learn about new things and it helps you be able to work with each other to grow.
### Problem Decomposition
Before I started to work on my game, I had so many ideas in my mind that I wanted to achieve. For example, I wanted the enemy targets to be able to target the shooting sprite and damage it's health bar. I had so many other ideas for my game to include even though I didn't know that much about kaboom. Then when it came time to work on my game, I realized that it was way harder than it seemed. Even the functions that looked simple took me a long time to learn and understand them so I learned to break everything down and learn everything step by step. For instance, I thought about learning how to make the sprite first, then learning how to make it move and so on. This skill was very beneficial to me while I was finishing my game as it helped me take on a large challenege by breaking the challenge into smaller parts.
